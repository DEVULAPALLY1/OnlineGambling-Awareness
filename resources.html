Types of Treatment

Professional help options

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps identify and change unhealthy gambling behaviors and thoughts, addressing cognitive distortions related to gambling.

Motivational Interviewing

This approach helps individuals resolve ambivalent feelings about gambling and find internal motivation to change.

Family Therapy

Involves family members in the recovery process, addressing relationship issues and establishing healthy boundaries.

Finding Treatment

Resources to locate professional help

  • TISS iCALL – Free mental health helpline by Tata Institute of Social Sciences : +919152987821 or visit icallhelpline.org
  • Psychology Today: Search for therapists specializing in gambling disorder at Check?
  • National Council on Problem Gambling: Find treatment providers at ncpgambling.org/help-treatment

Gamblers Anonymous

Peer support through 12-step program

Gamblers Anonymous is a fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope with each other to solve their common problem and help others recover from a gambling problem.

How to Find Meetings:

  • Visit gamblersanonymous.org
  • Call the GA hotline: 1-855-222-5542
  • Many meetings are now available online

Self-Help Strategies

Tools for managing gambling urges

Financial Controls

  • Give control of finances to a trusted person
  • Set up automatic bill payments
  • Close online gambling accounts
  • Use gambling blocking software on devices

Lifestyle Changes

  • Find alternative activities to replace gambling
  • Practice stress management techniques
  • Rebuild relationships damaged by gambling
  • Avoid triggers and high-risk situations

Self-Exclusion Programs

Many casinos and online gambling sites offer self-exclusion programs that prevent you from accessing their services for a specified period.
